SAP Implementation FAQ

Common questions about SAP deployments and strategy.

How long does an enterprise SAP implementation take

Timeline depends on scope, complexity, and number of modules. Typical implementations range from 6-18 months. We provide detailed plans with clear milestones and go-live readiness gates upfront.

Should we upgrade to S/4HANA or implement new

We assess your current landscape and roadmap to recommend the optimal path: brownfield upgrade for existing SAP customers or greenfield implementation. Both approaches reduce risks and maximize ROI.

How do we minimize disruption during cutover

We employ phased rollout approaches, parallel run strategies, comprehensive testing cycles, and detailed cutover plans with trained super-users to ensure smooth, low-risk go-live transitions.

What about data migration and validation

We establish comprehensive data governance, cleansing, and validation protocols. Multiple data migration cycles, reconciliation checks, and user acceptance testing ensure data integrity and accuracy.

How is user adoption managed across the enterprise

We develop change management programs, executive sponsorship models, comprehensive training curricula, super-user networks, and ongoing support to drive adoption and maximize system utilization.

What is the typical cost model for SAP implementation

We provide flexible engagement models: fixed-price for defined scope, time-and-material for evolving requirements, or managed services payback tied to realized benefits. Full transparency on costs and ROI timelines.
